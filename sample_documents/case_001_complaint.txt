                         COMPLAINT  FOR DAMAGES

Plaintiff MARCUS A. HENDRICKS ("Plaintiff"  or  "Hendricks")  hereby  alleges  as
follows against  Defendants NEXIGEN PHARMACEUTICALS, INC. ("Nexigen"),  RAYMOND L.
PORTER  ("Porter"),  and  DOES  1-20  (collectively  "Defendants"):

                            JURISDICTION  AND  VENUE

1.  This  Court  has  jurisdiction  over  the  subject matter  of  this  action
pursuant  to  California Code  of  Civil  Procedure §  410.10.  The  amount  in
controversy exceeds TWENTY-FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($25,000.00).

2.  Venue  is  proper  in  Los  Angeles  County  pursuant  to  CCP §  395(a)
because  Defendant  Nexigen  maintains  its  principal  place  of  business  at
1200  Century Park East,  Suite  1800,  Los  Angeles,  California  90067.

                               THE  PARTIES

3.  Plaintiff  Marcus A. Hendricks  is  a  resident  of  Los  Angeles  County,
California. At  all  times  relevant,  Hendricks  was  employed  as  Senior
Director  of  Business  Development  at  Nexigen  from  Apri1  2019  through
October  14,  2023  (note:  "Apri1" may be OCR  artifact  for  "April"  — original

4.  Defendant  NEXIGEN  PHARMACEUTICALS,  INC.  is  a  Delaware  corporation

the  address  stated  above.  Nexigen  develops  and  markets  oncology  and
rare-disease  therapeutics.

5.  Defendant  RAYMOND  L.  PORTER  is  an  individual  residing  in  [REDACTED
PER  PRIVACY  REQUEST],  California  and  is,  and  at  all  relevant  times
was,  the  Chief  Executive  Officer  of  Nexigen.

                            GENERAL  ALLEGATIONS

6.  On or  about  April  3,  2019,  Nexigen  and  Hendricks  entered  into  a
written  Employment  Agreement  ("Agreement")  pursuant  to  which  Hendricks
would  serve  as  Senior  Director  of  Business  Development  in  exchange  for:
(a)  an  annual  base  salary  of  $210,000;
(b)  eligibility  for  an  annual  performance  bonus  not  to  exceed  40%  of
     base  salary;
(c)  a  restricted  stock  unit  grant  of  150,000  shares  vesting  over  four
     (4)  years  (the  "RSU  Grant"); and
(d)  severance  equal  to  twelve  (12)  months  of  base  salary  upon
     termination  without  cause.

7.  Throughout  his  employment,  Hendricks  consistently  received  performance
ratings  of  "Exceeds  Expectations"  and  "Outstanding"  in  Nexigen's  annual
review  process  (see  Exhibit  A — Performance  Reviews  2019-2023,  attached).

8.  On  or  about  September  1,  2023,  Porter  orally  represented  to
Hendricks  that  Nexigen  planned  to  promote  Hendricks  to  Vice  President  of
Strategic  Partnerships,  with  a  base  salary  increase  to  $280,000,  effective
January  1,  2024.   These  representations  were  made  at  Nexigen  headquarters
in  Los  Angeles.

9.  In  reasonable  reliance  on  Porter's  representations,  Hendricks  declined
competitive  employment  offers  from  Roche  Holdings  AG  ($295,000  base)  and
Amgen  Inc.  ($310,000  base)  during  October  2023.

10.  On  October  14,  2023,  without  prior  warning  and  without  cause,
Nexigen  terminated  Hendricks'  employment  effective  immediately.  At  the  time
of  termination:
     —  Hendricks  had  112,500  unvested  RSUs  (75%  of  the  original  grant);
     —  Nexigen  refused  to  pay  severance  as  required  by  the  Agreement;
     —  Nexigen  refused  to  accelerate  vesting  of  RSUs  as  required  upon
        termination  without  cause.

                            FIRST CAUSE  OF  ACTION
                        (Breach  of  Contract — All  Defendants)

13.  Plaintiff  incorporates  paragraphs  1-12  by  reference.

14.  The  Employment  Agreement  is  a  valid,  enforceable  contract.   Hendricks
performed  all  conditions  on  his  part.   Defendants  breached  the  Agreement
by:
     (i)   failing  to  pay  severance  upon  termination  without  cause;
     (ii)  forfeiting  Hendricks'  unvested  RSUs  without  contractual
           justification;
     (iii) failing  to  provide  the  contractually  required  30-day  notice
           period.

15.  As  a  direct  and  proximate  result  of  Defendants'  breach,  Hendricks
has  suffered  damages  in  an  amount  to  be  proven  at  trial,  but  not  less
than  $2,704,125  (comprising  $210,000  severance  +  $2,494,125  RSU  value).
